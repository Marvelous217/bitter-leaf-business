Why it matters

Good for the pot, good for the body.

Bitter Leaf

Long used across West Africa to support digestion and settle the stomach. It's rich in fibre and plant compounds that many households rely on as part of a balanced diet, alongside its role in easing everyday aches.

Scent Leaf

Known for its strong aroma and its place in traditional remedies for colds and fevers. It's also valued for supporting healthy digestion and is a natural way to add flavour without relying on extra salt or seasoning cubes.

Fluted Pumpkin Leaf

A good source of vitamins A and C, iron, and fibre. Regularly included in soups and stews, it's known to support the immune system and is often recommended for its blood-building properties.

Fluted Pumpkin Seed

Packed with protein, healthy fats, zinc, and magnesium. A handful goes a long way for muscle and immune support, and it's a filling, natural addition to soups once processed.

This is general food knowledge, not medical advice — if you have a health condition, check with a doctor about what fits your diet.
